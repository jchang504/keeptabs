KeepTabs Options

Set hold key

The hold key is the key you hold while typing to activate your hotkeys. If it is used for native shortcuts in Chrome (e.g. Ctrl+f on Windows), creating an overlapping hotkey may or may not override the native shortcut. Operating system shortcuts, however, will not get overridden, so choose your hold key carefully to avoid side effects.

Customize hotkeys

The domain saved here will have a shortcut to switch to or create the corresponding tab (e.g. facebook.com, google.com). The HotKey entry is linked to which hotkeys pressed will navigate you to the corresponding domain (e.g. "f" to open facebook). Checking the Deduplicate option will bring you back to an existing window if you have one already opened.
